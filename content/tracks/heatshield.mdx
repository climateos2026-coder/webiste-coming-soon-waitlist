## Why HeatShield Matters

Twelve major cities now regularly exceed 48°C at peak summer. Urban heat islands in dense metros run 5–7°C warmer than surrounding rural areas. Most heat action plans are policy documents — they are not operational tools that a ward-level officer or community health worker can use in the thirty minutes before a vulnerable elderly resident's life is at risk.

## The Challenge

Build an open-source, deployable tool that helps a ward-level municipal officer or community health worker identify the three highest-risk micro-zones in their area during an active heat event, recommend one immediate intervention (activate a cooling centre, alert a vulnerable household cluster, trigger a water distribution unit), and track the outcome.

The tool must work on a 3G mobile connection. It must surface actionable intelligence — not raw data — to a user who may not have a technical background. And it must be free to deploy.

## What Success Looks Like

- A ward officer opens your tool on their phone and gets a prioritised risk map in under 30 seconds
- The tool recommends a specific intervention with a rationale
- Intervention outcomes are logged so the tool learns over time
